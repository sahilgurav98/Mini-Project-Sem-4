The canteen operates from Monday to Saturday, between 8:00 AM and 5:00 PM. It remains closed on Sundays and public holidays.
Breakfast is served from 8:00 AM to 11:00 AM. Lunch is available from 12:00 PM to 3:00 PM. Evening snacks are served between 3:30 PM and 5:00 PM.
The canteen is located on the ground floor of the engineering main building, right next to the student recreation center.
If a student forgets their password, they can click on 'Forgot Password' on the login screen. They will need to provide their registered email address.
During the password reset process, users must enter a new password and confirm it in the 'Confirm Password' field to ensure no typos were made.
Students can update their profile information, including dietary preferences, from the settings page inside the Student Dashboard.
Once an order is placed on the dashboard, it is placed in a 'Pending' queue. A student can cancel their order within 5 minutes of placing it without any penalty.
If an order is already marked as 'Preparing' or a coupon has been issued by the admin, it cannot be canceled.
Refunds for canceled pre-orders are processed back to the student's digital wallet within 24 hours. The canteen does not provide physical cash refunds for online wallet transactions.
A standard veg thali costs ₹60 and includes 2 rotis, a vegetable curry, dal, rice, and a sweet. It is the most popular item during lunch hours.
Snacks such as Samosas (₹15), Vada Pav (₹15), and Poha (₹20) are available all day but are in highest demand during morning and evening breaks.
Beverages include Tea (₹10), Coffee (₹15), and Cold Drinks (₹20). Cold drinks are subject to availability.
During campus fests or specific college events, the admin toggles the 'Event Status' on the dashboard. This allows the AI to adjust predictions for higher foot traffic.
On event days, the canteen offers a specialized fast-food menu to handle high crowd volumes quickly, temporarily pausing complex thali preparation.
Students can track the real-time status of their food orders directly from their dashboard, keeping them updated during busy event days.
The machine learning model's performance is measured using Mean Absolute Error (MAE), which represents the average difference between the predicted food demand and the actual sales.
The MAE metric is prominently displayed on the Admin Dashboard so the canteen manager can monitor how accurate the AI predictions are.
As the AI gathers more daily sales data over time, it retrains itself during off-hours, continuously lowering the MAE and making estimations more accurate.

Admin Manual - Viewing Orders: The Admin Dashboard displays all incoming student orders in real-time. Orders initially appear in the 'Pending' state.
Admin Manual - Order Verification: When a student pays at the physical counter, the admin must locate their order on the dashboard and click 'Issue Coupon'. This verifies payment and moves the order to the kitchen's list.
Admin Manual - Order Status Updates: Once the food is ready, the admin updates the order status to 'Ready'. The student will receive a notification on their dashboard to pick up their food.
Admin Manual - Event Status: The admin has access to an 'Event Status' toggle on the dashboard. During college fests, holidays, or special gatherings, the admin must turn this toggle ON. 
Admin Manual - Why Event Status Matters: Turning on the Event Status informs the Machine Learning model that foot traffic will be anomalous, preventing the AI from under-predicting food demand for that day.
Admin Manual - AI Predictions: The admin can view tomorrow's predicted food demand directly on the dashboard. This tells the kitchen staff exactly how many units of each item to prepare.
Admin Manual - Checking MAE: The dashboard displays the Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of the Machine Learning model. A lower MAE means the AI's food predictions are highly accurate. If the MAE gets too high, the admin should trigger a model retraining.
Admin Manual - Training the AI: The admin can click the 'Train Model' button at the end of the week. This feeds the latest order history into the neural network, helping it learn changing student preferences.
